Lesson 1: Division as Sharing

Division splits a total into equal groups. 12 ÷ 3 asks: 'If I share 12 things into 3 equal groups, how many in each?' Answer: 4. Division 'undoes' multiplication.

Lesson 2: Fact Families

A fact family is a set of related multiplication/division facts using the same three numbers. Example: 4, 5, and 20 → 4×5=20, 5×4=20, 20÷4=5, 20÷5=4. If you know one, you know all four!

Lesson 3: Division Word Problems

Look for phrases like 'shared equally,' 'split into groups,' or 'each got the same.' These signal division. Ask yourself: what's the total, and how many groups?
