Curriculum Map by Quarter

Each unit lists its standards, learning objectives, essential content, pacing, assessments, and resources.

Unit Standards Standards Reference Learning Objectives Essential Content & Skills Sequencing & Pacing Assessments Resources Used
Reading: Building a Reading Life Std CCSS RL.3.1-10; RF.3.4; RI.3.5 1. Establish reading routines; 2. Apply 5 comprehension strategies; 3. Track thinking with sticky notes. Visualizing, inferring, predicting, questioning, summarizing. Read-aloud novel: Muggie Maggie. Q1: 9 weeks (8/3–10/2) Reading journal; Novel comprehension test. Muggie Maggie by Beverly Cleary; ReadWorks; CommonLit.
Reading: Reading to Learn (Nonfiction) Std CCSS RI.3.1-10; RF.3.4 1. Identify main ideas; 2. Use nonfiction text structures; 3. Compare accounts of same event. Expository text structure; text features (headings, glossary, captions); main idea and details. Q2: 9 weeks (10/19–12/18) Nonfiction summary; Text structure quiz. What Was the First Thanksgiving? by Joan Holub; National Geographic Kids.
Reading: Character Study Std CCSS RL.3.1-6 1. Analyze character traits and motives; 2. Find central message; 3. Discuss story elements. Story elements (characters, setting, plot); analyzing character traits and motives; theme. Q3: 9 weeks (1/7–3/12) Character analysis essay; Novel test. Family Under the Bridge by Natalie Savage Carlson.
Reading: Mystery — Skills in Disguise Std CCSS RL.3.1-3; RI.3.1 1. Track clues and evidence; 2. Infer and predict; 3. Notice details. Story details; inference; prediction; clue-tracking notebooks. Q4: 9 weeks (3/29–6/3) Clue journal; Mystery unit test. A-Z Mysteries: The White Wolf by Ron Roy.
Writing: Launching Workshop & True Stories Std CCSS W.3.3 (Narratives); W.3.5 (Revise/edit); L.3.1-3 1. Draft small-moment personal narratives; 2. Use dialogue and sensory details; 3. Revise and edit. Writing workshop routines; small moments; author's craft. Q1: 9 weeks (8/3–10/2) Published personal narrative; Writing rubric. Lucy Calkins Units of Study.
Writing: Informational Writing Std CCSS W.3.2 (Informative); W.3.7-8 (Research) 1. Research a topic; 2. Structure informational text; 3. Cite sources. Text features; topic sentences; research skills; All-About books. Q2: 9 weeks (10/19–12/18) All-About book publication. Encyclopedia Britannica Kids; PebbleGo.
Writing: Persuasive Writing Std CCSS W.3.1 (Opinion); L.3.1-3 1. Distinguish opinion from fact; 2. Support claims with reasons and evidence; 3. Use linking words. Opinion vs fact; reasons and evidence; persuasive letters and ads. Q3: 9 weeks (1/7–3/12) Persuasive letter; Ad-poster project. Common Sense Media; ad archives.
Writing: Poetry & Adapting Fairy Tales Std CCSS W.3.3; W.3.10; L.3.5 1. Read and write poems; 2. Use figurative language; 3. Adapt classic fairy tales. Rhyme, rhythm, alliteration, metaphor, simile; haiku, free verse; fairy tale structure. Q4: 9 weeks (3/29–6/3) Poetry anthology; Fairy-tale adaptation. Shel Silverstein; Grimm fairy tales; Poetry Foundation.
Grammar & Phonics (weekly across all quarters) Std CCSS L.3.1-2; RF.3.3-4 1. Master parts of speech; 2. Apply punctuation and capitalization rules; 3. Decode long vowels, blends, endings. Nouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs, pronouns, conjunctions; commas, quotations, possessives; long/short vowel patterns; digraphs and blends; prefixes/suffixes; plurals; compounds; homonyms. All Year: 36 weeks (8/3–6/3) Phonics quizzes; grammar assessed within composition and writing. IXL; SpellingCity; Words Their Way.
