Unit Standards Standards Reference Learning Objectives Essential Content & Skills Sequencing & Pacing Assessments Resources Used
Topic 1-3: Multiplication Foundations Std 3.OA.1 (Interpret products); 3.OA.3 (Multiplication word problems); 3.OA.5 (Properties); 3.OA.7 (Facts fluency); 3.OA.9 (Patterns) 1. Interpret products as equal groups; 2. Fluently multiply within 100 using patterns; 3. Apply commutative, associative, and distributive properties. Understand multiplication as equal groups (Topic 1); Fact patterns for 2, 5, 9, 10 (Topic 2); Properties for facts 3, 4, 6, 7, 8 (Topic 3). Skip counting, arrays, area models. Q1: 9 weeks (8/3–10/2) Topic quizzes 1, 2, 3; Multiplication fact fluency test. enVisionMATH 2.0; Khan Academy; Math Antics videos; Multiplication.com games.
Topic 4-5: Division Facts Std 3.OA.2 (Interpret quotients); 3.OA.4 (Determine unknown numbers); 3.OA.6 (Division as unknown factor); 3.OA.7 (Fluent multiply/divide within 100) 1. Understand division as an unknown-factor problem; 2. Master fact families; 3. Fluently divide within 100. Division as sharing and grouping (Topic 4); Fact families; Fluent multiplication and division within 100 (Topic 5). Q2: 4 weeks (10/19–11/13) Topic quizzes 4, 5; Fact family mastery check. enVisionMATH 2.0; Khan Academy; Xtramath; timed fluency drills.
Topic 6-7: Area & Data Std 3.MD.3 (Draw graphs); 3.MD.4 (Line plots); 3.MD.5-7 (Area concepts) 1. Compute area of rectangles as L × W; 2. Draw and interpret picture graphs, bar graphs, and line plots. Area = length × width; unit squares; distributive property with area; picture, bar, and line-plot data displays. Q2: 5 weeks (11/16–12/18) Topic quizzes 6, 7; Data project. enVisionMATH 2.0; graph paper; sticky-dot line plots; NCTM data lessons.
Topic 8-9: Add & Subtract Strategies + Fluency Std 3.NBT.2 (Fluently add/sub within 1,000) 1. Use place-value strategies for addition and subtraction; 2. Fluently add and subtract within 1,000 using the standard algorithm. Break-apart, compensation, counting-on strategies; regrouping algorithm; error checking with reverse operation. Q3: 4 weeks (1/7–2/5) Topic quizzes 8, 9; 1,000-number fluency check. enVisionMATH 2.0; base-10 blocks; place value charts.
Topic 10-11: Multiply by 10s & Word Problems Std 3.NBT.3 (Multiply by multiples of 10); 3.OA.8 (Two-step word problems) 1. Multiply single digits by multiples of 10; 2. Solve two-step word problems using all four operations. ×10, ×20, …, ×90 patterns; two-step word problems with reasonableness checks. Q3: 3 weeks (2/8–2/26) Topic quizzes 10, 11; Multi-step problem-solving assessment. enVisionMATH 2.0; word-problem journals.
Topic 15: Attributes of 2D Shapes Std 3.G.1 (Classify quadrilaterals); 3.G.2 (Partition shapes) 1. Classify quadrilaterals by attributes; 2. Partition shapes into equal areas. Squares, rectangles, rhombuses, parallelograms, trapezoids; hierarchy of shapes; area partitioning. Q3: 2 weeks (3/1–3/12) Topic 15 quiz; Shape-classifying project. enVisionMATH 2.0; pattern blocks; geoboards.
Topic 12-13: Fractions Intro & Equivalence Std 3.NF.1 (Unit fractions); 3.NF.2 (Fractions on number line); 3.NF.3 (Equivalent fractions and comparison) 1. Understand fractions as numbers; 2. Locate fractions on a number line; 3. Recognize and generate equivalent fractions. Numerator/denominator; unit fractions; fractions on number lines; equivalence rules; simplifying; comparing. Q4: 5 weeks (3/29–4/30) Topic quizzes 12, 13; Fraction number-line assessment. enVisionMATH 2.0; fraction bars; number lines; NLVM manipulatives.
Topic 14: Time, Capacity & Mass Std 3.MD.1 (Elapsed time); 3.MD.2 (Liquid volumes and masses) 1. Measure elapsed time to the nearest minute; 2. Measure and estimate liquid volume (mL, L) and mass (g, kg). Clock reading, elapsed time on number lines; liter and milliliter; gram and kilogram; measurement word problems. Q4: 2 weeks (5/3–5/14) Topic 14 quiz; Measurement lab report. enVisionMATH 2.0; clocks, measuring cups, balance scales.
Topic 16: Perimeter Std 3.MD.8 (Perimeter of polygons) 1. Compute perimeter of polygons; 2. Find unknown side lengths given perimeter; 3. Distinguish perimeter from area. Perimeter formulas (P = 2L + 2W for rectangles, P = 4s for squares); missing-side problems; comparing shapes with equal perimeter but different areas. Q4: 2 weeks (5/17–6/3) Topic 16 quiz; End-of-year cumulative review. enVisionMATH 2.0; grid paper; string and rulers.
