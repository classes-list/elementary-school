Lesson 1: Classify Quadrilaterals

A quadrilateral is any 4-sided shape. Special quadrilaterals: SQUARES (4 equal sides, 4 right angles), RECTANGLES (4 right angles), RHOMBUSES (4 equal sides), PARALLELOGRAMS (opposite sides parallel), TRAPEZOIDS (1 pair of parallel sides).

4. A rhombus has how many equal sides?
5. A trapezoid has how many pairs of parallel sides?
6. A parallelogram has how many pairs of parallel sides?

Lesson 2: Shape Hierarchy

Every square IS a rectangle (4 right angles). Every square IS a rhombus (4 equal sides). But not every rectangle is a square. Shapes belong to overlapping families!

📝 Practice Worksheet

Try 6 problems. Type each answer, then click Check.

1. Is every square a rectangle? (1=yes, 0=no)
2. Is every rectangle a square?
3. Is every square a rhombus?
4. Is every rhombus a square?
5. Is every rectangle a parallelogram?
6. Is every rhombus a parallelogram?

Lesson 3: Partitioning Shapes

You can split a shape into equal parts to model fractions. Split a rectangle into 4 equal strips and each strip is 1/4 of the shape. Splitting must give EQUAL parts.

6. Split into 5 = 1/?
