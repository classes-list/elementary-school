Lesson 1: Multiplication as Equal Groups

Multiplication is a way of counting equal groups quickly. If you have 3 baskets and each basket holds 4 apples, that's 3 groups of 4, or 3 × 4 = 12 apples. The first number tells you HOW MANY groups. The second number tells you HOW MANY IN EACH group.

Lesson 2: Arrays and Rows

An array shows equal groups as rows and columns. A 3-row-by-4-column array = 3 × 4 = 12. You can read the same array as rows × columns OR columns × rows — the total is the same. Arrays help you SEE why multiplication works.

Lesson 3: Skip Counting to Multiply

Skip counting is repeated addition in disguise! Counting by 5s (5, 10, 15, 20...) is the same as adding 5 over and over. Skip counting by 5 four times gives you 5 × 4 = 20. Use skip counting to check your multiplication.

Lesson 4: Multiplication Word Problems

Word problems tell a story. Read carefully to find HOW MANY groups and HOW MANY per group. Look for words like 'each,' 'every,' 'per,' 'rows of.' Draw a quick picture if you get stuck.
