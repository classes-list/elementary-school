Lesson 1: Perimeter of Polygons

Perimeter is the DISTANCE AROUND a shape. Add up all the sides. For a rectangle 4 by 6: 4+6+4+6 = 20. For a square with side 5: 5+5+5+5 = 20 (or 4 × 5). Include the UNIT.

Lesson 2: Missing Side Lengths

If perimeter is given and one side is missing, work backwards. Rectangle: P = 20, one side = 4. Two 4s use up 8. So the two other sides together = 12, meaning each = 6.

Lesson 3: Perimeter vs. Area

Perimeter measures AROUND (a fence). Area measures INSIDE (a floor). Two shapes can have the same area but different perimeters — and vice versa. Different measurements answer different questions.
