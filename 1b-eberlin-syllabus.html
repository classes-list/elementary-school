📝 Homework and Study Expectations

Following the MSOS guidelines of ~10 minutes per grade, first graders should expect roughly 10–15 minutes of homework per night, plus daily independent reading. Homework is meant to reinforce (not introduce) what was learned in class.

  • Each day your child will bring home their Home-to-School Folder and Home-to-School Agenda Book. Please check both the folder and initial the agenda book daily. Return to school each day.
  • Your child’s homework log for the week will be glued into their Home-to-School Agenda Book.
  • The homework log will indicate the homework each day and note important dates/reminders.
  • Homework sheets will go home in their folder (Monday–Thursday). Homework sheets will be double-sided with math and language arts work. No homework is assigned on Fridays or during school breaks.
  • Please remove all old paperwork daily. You will know I have checked homework if there is a star or happy face at the top.
  • Monthly calendar and lunch schedule in sheet protectors. Please leave these documents in the folder.

Students will also be receiving reading folders with books at their reading level. These will be sent home starting in September. Reading folders go home on Monday with books for the week. Books are to be returned in the reading folder at the end of the school week in order to get a new set of books on Monday.

To support homework at home:

  • Set a consistent, quiet study time and place.
  • Read the directions together, then step back and let your child work independently.
  • Encourage your child to do their best work.
  • If a task takes much longer than expected or your child is struggling, please let me know.

🤝 Classroom Procedures and Behavior Expectations

Our class has created a “classroom contract” that establishes how we’d like to feel while at school and the actions that should be taken to make everyone in our class feel like a part of our community. The students participated in a collaborative discussion in which we agreed upon the actions we wanted to see in the classroom contract. Each action fell under the umbrella terms of safety, attentiveness, respect, kindness, and doing your best. Each student is expected to follow the agreed-upon expectations that were established during this conversation.

Other classroom expectations will be explicitly taught and demonstrated daily. Students are expected to work to high standards but are treated with warmth, respect, and fairness. I will contact you directly if your student is having any difficulties following classroom expectations.

  • 1. Be Safe
  • 2. Be Attentive
  • 3. Be Respectful
  • 4. Be Kind
  • 5. Do your Best

When expectations are not met (in a supportive tone):

  1. Reminder and reset
  2. Reflection sheet or think time
  3. Loss of a small privilege
  4. Phone call home and, if needed, a family meeting with the principal

📊 Assessment and Grading

Daily class work and homework will be checked for thoroughness, effort, and neatness. Writing assessments and class projects will be graded using rubrics. I will frequently be sitting down with students one-on-one to formally assess their academic progress. Throughout the school day I use informal, ongoing assessments to track student academic growth throughout the unit. I will then use summative assessments at the end of a unit or topic to measure student growth. Report cards are given at the end of each quarter and are graded using a 1 to 4 scale.

1
Beginning
  • Beginning to understand the concept or skill
  • Requires significant support and guidance
  • Limited understanding or mastery of the topic
2
Developing
  • Demonstrates partial understanding or skill
  • Needs occasional assistance or support
  • Progressing but with notable gaps
3
Proficient
  • Meets grade-level expectations
  • Demonstrates a solid understanding of concepts or skills
  • Works independently with minimal errors
4
Mastery
  • Exceeds grade-level expectations
  • Demonstrates thorough understanding and can apply skills in new situations
  • Work is consistently accurate and shows higher-level thinking

Report cards are based on how your child performs each quarter. Grades are not reflected based on year-end benchmarks. Students also take a standardized assessment called STAR for early literacy and math in August and May.

🏠 How To Support Your Child At Home

Families are our most important partners in helping students succeed. The routines and habits established at home provide the foundation for strong first-grade learners and make a meaningful difference in your child’s learning, confidence, and overall success. Children thrive when they have a consistent bedtime, arrive at school on time, read with an adult each day, complete homework, and talk with their family about their school day.

Encouraging independence and responsibility through simple daily tasks — such as packing their backpack, caring for their belongings, and taking ownership of their work — helps build confidence and self-reliance. Equally important is maintaining open communication with your child’s teacher, creating a strong home-school partnership that supports every child’s growth. By working together to establish these routines, foster responsibility, and stay connected, we can help every first grader become a confident, capable, and successful learner.
