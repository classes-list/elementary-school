✝️ Religion

The Four Marks of the Church · SY 2026-2027

Course theme: \"I believe in one, holy, catholic, and apostolic Church.\" — The Nicene Creed
Our 3rd graders explore the four marks of the Church and how she carries the mission of Christ to the world.
Q1
Unit 1
The Church is One

Big Ideas

  • The Church is one Body, united in Christ
  • Jesus founded ONE Church, not many separate ones
  • Unity comes from shared faith, sacraments, and leadership

What Students Learn

  • The Trinity: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit
  • The Body of Christ (1 Cor 12)
  • How Baptism unites us into the Church
  • The role of the Pope as visible sign of unity
  • Ecumenism — working with other Christians
Prayer focus: Sign of the Cross, Our Father
Q2
Unit 2
The Church is Holy

Big Ideas

  • The Church is holy because Christ is holy
  • The Holy Spirit makes us holy through the sacraments
  • Saints show us how to live holy lives

What Students Learn

  • The seven sacraments (with focus on Baptism, Eucharist, Reconciliation)
  • The Holy Spirit's gifts and fruits
  • The Ten Commandments
  • The Beatitudes
  • Lives of the saints: St. Damien of Molokai, St. Marianne Cope (Hawaii connection)
  • Feast days and the liturgical calendar
Prayer focus: Hail Mary, Glory Be, Act of Contrition
Q3
Unit 3
The Church is Catholic

Big Ideas

  • "Catholic" means universal — for all people, everywhere
  • The Church welcomes people of every language, culture, and place
  • Jesus calls all people to be his disciples

What Students Learn

  • The Great Commission (Matthew 28:19-20)
  • The Church across cultures — Africa, Asia, Americas, Europe
  • The role of missionaries throughout history
  • The universal call to holiness
  • Catholic Social Teaching principles (love of neighbor, care for the poor)
  • Introduction to the Mass and its universal structure
Prayer focus: Nicene Creed, prayers in other languages
Q4
Unit 4
The Church is Apostolic

Big Ideas

  • The Church was founded on the apostles by Jesus Christ
  • Apostolic succession — bishops trace their line back to the apostles
  • We receive faith from Jesus through the apostles and their successors

What Students Learn

  • The Twelve Apostles — who they were and what they did
  • St. Peter as the first Pope
  • The role of bishops, priests, and deacons today
  • How Scripture and Tradition together pass on the faith
  • The story of Pentecost (Acts 2)
  • Missionary journeys of St. Paul
Prayer focus: Apostles' Creed, Come Holy Spirit
Bonus
Unit 5
The Church Has a Mission to the World

Big Ideas

  • The Church exists to bring Christ's love to the world
  • Every Christian is called to be a missionary in daily life
  • Corporal and spiritual works of mercy shape our mission

What Students Learn

  • Corporal Works of Mercy (feed the hungry, clothe the naked, etc.)
  • Spiritual Works of Mercy (instruct, counsel, pray)
  • Service projects: how can 3rd graders help?
  • Stewardship of God's creation (Laudato Si' for kids)
  • Being a witness at school, home, and community
  • Mary, Star of the Sea, as guide for our mission
Prayer focus: Prayer of St. Francis, Prayer to Mary, Star of the Sea
