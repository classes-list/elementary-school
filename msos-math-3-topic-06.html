Lesson 1: Counting Unit Squares

Area is how much SPACE a flat shape covers. We measure area in unit squares. A rectangle 3 units wide and 4 units tall covers 12 unit squares. Count them or multiply — same answer.

Lesson 2: Area of Rectangles

Area = length × width. If a rug is 5 ft long and 4 ft wide, area = 5 × 4 = 20 square feet. Always include the UNIT: square feet, square meters, square inches. Area answers 'how much surface?'

Lesson 3: Area with Distributive Property

Big rectangles can be split. A 6 × 8 rectangle = a 6×5 piece + a 6×3 piece = 30 + 18 = 48. This is the distributive property helping with area!

Lesson 4: Area Word Problems

Real areas: floors, gardens, walls, screens. Read for length and width, multiply, add units. If a problem gives area and one side, DIVIDE to find the other side.
