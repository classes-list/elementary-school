💜 Mrs. Won's Teaching Philosophy

My teaching philosophy is rooted in nurturing the development of the whole child — socially, emotionally, and academically. I believe that every child learns best in a safe and supportive environment where they feel empowered to take risks, make mistakes, and grow from them. It is in this space that we nurture curiosity, unite as a classroom family, and inspire one another to reach our God-given potential.

Equally important is helping students grow in their relationship with God. Through daily prayer, reflection, and faith-filled learning, I hope to guide each child to become the unique person God created them to be.

At Mary, Star of the Sea School, we guide our students to become S.T.A.R.s:

  • ⭐ Self-aware, Confident Individuals
  • ⭐ Teachable, Lifelong Independent Learners
  • ⭐ Active Christians
  • ⭐ Responsible Citizens

My goal is to help your child grow in all these areas by building a strong foundation for both their academic journey and their life of faith. Together, we will nurture hearts and minds, unite in purpose and spirit, and inspire one another each day. I am excited for this new beginning as we grow together — empowered to succeed and always guided by faith.

📊 Grading

Grading will be based on a variety of daily classwork, projects, assessments, and homework. Late work will be accepted but may affect the student's study skills grade. Quality work is also expected (including manuscript-cursive writing).

Primary Grades K-3 Report Card Grading:

1
Beginning
  • The student is beginning to understand the concept or skill
  • Requires significant support and guidance
  • Limited understanding or mastery of the topic
2
Developing
  • The student demonstrates partial understanding or skill
  • Needs occasional assistance or support
  • Progressing but with notable gaps
3
Proficient
  • The student meets grade-level expectations
  • Demonstrates a solid understanding of concepts or skills
  • Works independently with minimal errors
4
Mastery
  • The student exceeds grade-level expectations
  • Demonstrates a thorough understanding and can apply skills in new situations
  • Work is consistently accurate and shows higher-level thinking

Report cards are based on how your child performs each quarter. Students also take a standardized assessment called STAR Renaissance for Math and Literacy in fall, winter, and spring. English Language Learners take the WIDA assessment as required.

Parent-Teacher-Student Conferences are held at the end of the first and third quarters.

📝 Homework and Study Expectations

ALL homework is a review of concepts taught in class / “Choice Boards” (see details below). Projects will periodically be assigned. Additional reviews / incomplete assignments will be added to daily homework as necessary. Students' Homework Agendas to be signed DAILY.

Choice Board Project and book report will be assigned twice a quarter, with 4 weeks to complete.

What is a Choice Board?

  • A menu or grid of learning activities
  • Students choose which activities to complete
  • Covers different subjects / concepts / skills
  • To be completed at home

Purpose of a Choice Board for Homework:

  • Gives students voice and choice
  • Makes homework more fun and engaging
  • Supports different learning styles
  • Encourages responsibility and independence
  • *Reinforces subjects, concepts, and skills taught in class
  • Allows for creativity and deeper thinking

⭐ Third Grade Homework Guidelines ⭐

(See Student Agenda for more details.)

3rd Graders are expected to spend about 30 minutes per night with about 20 minutes of independent reading. All homework is meant to reinforce concepts and skills learned in class.

  1. 📘 Check your Student Agenda book every day after school to see what homework needs to be done.

💛 Wellness

Wellness for third grade is once a week during our third quarter with our School Counselor, Ms. Tacy.

Students will explore their self-awareness by recognizing and managing their emotions.

Brief description of each week of 3rd-grade SEL/Wellness lessons:

  1. The Mood Meter — introduce it, scale, and name feelings
  2. Feelings Detectives — What is empathy? Reading others' emotions; we can feel different emotions about the same thing
  3. Thought Connections — How thoughts relate to feelings and actions; caterpillar thoughts (positive, growth-oriented) vs. worm thoughts (negative); growth mindset
  4. What makes a good friend? Finding commonalities and identifying strengths in self and others
  5. Stereotypes — what they are and how they can be harmful
  6. Battle the Bullies — what bullying is, reporting, assertiveness
  7. Problem-Solving Steps — stop, think, cool off, weigh consequences, take action
  8. Talk it out and clear it up — how to resolve conflicts, apologize, make new choices next time
  9. Transition to Intermediate — growing, changing, and learning in new ways
