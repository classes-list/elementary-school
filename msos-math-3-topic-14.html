Lesson 1: Elapsed Time

Elapsed time is how long something LASTS. Count forward from the start time to the end time. Use a number line: jump by hours first, then minutes. 3:15 to 4:45 = 1 hour 30 min.

2. From 2:00 to 2:30 = ? min
3. From 3:15 to 4:00 = ? min
4. From 5:00 to 5:45 = ? min
5. From 4:30 to 5:30 = ? min
6. From 1:15 to 2:00 = ? min

Lesson 2: Liquid Volume (mL, L)

Volume measures how much LIQUID a container holds. Small stuff: milliliters (mL). Big stuff: liters (L). 1 liter = 1,000 mL. A juice box is about 200 mL. A big soda bottle is 2 L.

📝 Practice Worksheet

Try 6 problems. Type each answer, then click Check.

2. How many mL in 2 L?
3. How many mL in 3 L?
4. 2 L + 500 mL = ? mL
5. 1 L + 250 mL = ? mL
6. 5 L = ? mL

Lesson 3: Mass (g, kg)

Mass measures how HEAVY something is. Small stuff: grams (g). Big stuff: kilograms (kg). 1 kg = 1,000 g. A paper clip ≈ 1 g. A math book ≈ 1 kg.

📝 Practice Worksheet

Try 6 problems. Type each answer, then click Check.

3. How many g in 5 kg?
4. 3 kg + 250 g = ? g
5. 1 kg + 500 g = ? g
6. 4 kg = ? g
