Lesson 1: Mixed Fact Practice

Fluency means you know the fact WITHOUT having to think about it. When someone says '7 × 8' — 56 pops into your head. Get there through practice: 10 minutes a day beats 1 hour once a week.

Lesson 2: Missing Numbers

Sometimes a problem hides a factor: 4 × ? = 32. Ask yourself: 'What times 4 gives 32?' Or use division: 32 ÷ 4 = 8. Missing number problems build the two-way link between × and ÷.

Lesson 3: Real-World Fluency

Use your fact fluency for real problems: prices, arrays, sharing snacks, packing supplies. When you don't have to stop and count, you can focus on the STORY of the problem.
