Q1
Civics
Unit 1: Communities Build a Nation
EQ: How does our past affect our future?

Big Ideas

  • Communities are groups of people living, working, and celebrating together
  • Leaders help communities make decisions and solve problems
  • Rules and laws help keep people safe and treat them fairly

What Students Learn

  • Types of communities: rural, suburban, urban
  • Roles of local leaders (mayor, police, teachers)
  • Rights and responsibilities of citizens
  • How governments are formed and why
  • Symbols of the United States (flag, eagle, monuments)
Key vocabulary: community, citizen, leader, rule, law, rights, responsibility, government
Q2
History
Unit 2: Governments
EQ: How do people organize themselves?

Big Ideas

  • People through history have formed governments to organize communities
  • Different governments (local, state, federal) have different jobs
  • The Constitution outlines the American plan of government

What Students Learn

  • Three branches of government (executive, legislative, judicial)
  • Purposes of the Constitution and Bill of Rights
  • How laws are made
  • Historic figures: Washington, Lincoln, King Kamehameha
  • Hawaiian government (unique history as a kingdom, then U.S. state)
Key vocabulary: constitution, branch, president, congress, judge, Bill of Rights, monarch, republic
Q3
Economics
Unit 3: Economics
EQ: How can we meet our needs in a changing world?

Big Ideas

  • People need goods and services to live and thrive
  • Producers make goods; consumers buy them
  • Trade, money, and savings help meet needs

What Students Learn

  • Needs vs wants
  • Goods vs services
  • Producers and consumers
  • How money works (currency, coins, banking)
  • Saving, spending, and budgeting
  • Trade between local, national, and global markets
  • Scarcity: what happens when there isn't enough
Key vocabulary: economy, goods, services, producer, consumer, currency, trade, scarcity, budget
Q4
Geography
Unit 4: Earth's Resources & Cultures Around the World
EQ: How do we live where we do?

Big Ideas

  • Geography shapes how people live, eat, dress, and work
  • Earth's natural resources are limited and must be used wisely
  • Cultures around the world express identity through language, food, art, and traditions

What Students Learn

  • The seven continents and five oceans
  • Reading maps: compass, legend, scale
  • Landforms: mountains, plains, rivers, deserts
  • Climate zones and how they shape communities
  • Natural resources: water, forests, minerals, farmland
  • Conservation and renewable resources
  • Cultural diversity: exploring 3-4 cultures in depth
  • Hawaiian culture: geography of the islands, native traditions, mo'olelo (stories)
Key vocabulary: continent, ocean, landform, climate, resource, conservation, culture, tradition, indigenous
