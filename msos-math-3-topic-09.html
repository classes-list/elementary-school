Lesson 1: Standard Addition Algorithm

Line numbers up by place value. Add ones first. If ones sum to 10 or more, carry a 1 to the tens. Then add tens (and any carry). Then hundreds. Keep place-value columns tidy.

Lesson 2: Standard Subtraction with Regrouping

When the top digit is smaller than the bottom, BORROW from the next column. 300 − 128: can't do 0−8, so borrow from tens (also 0, borrow from hundreds first). Rewrite as 2 hundreds, 9 tens, 10 ones = 172.

Lesson 3: Checking with Reverse Operations

Every subtraction problem can be checked with addition. 523 − 217 = 306. Check: 306 + 217 should equal 523. If yes, you're right. If no, redo the problem.
