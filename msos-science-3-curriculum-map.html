Curriculum Map by Quarter

Each unit lists its standards, learning objectives, essential content, pacing, assessments, and resources.

Unit Standards Standards Reference Learning Objectives Essential Content & Skills Sequencing & Pacing Assessments Resources Used
Scientific Method Std NGSS 3-5 Engineering Design; SEP: Asking Questions, Planning Investigations 1. Ask testable questions; 2. Form hypotheses; 3. Design fair tests; 4. Record and analyze data. 6 steps of scientific method; observation vs inference; variables; data recording; drawing conclusions. Q1: 4 weeks (8/3–8/28) Bounce Ball Investigation report; Scientific method quiz. PhET simulations; lab notebooks; NGSS resources.
Engineering Design & Technology Std NGSS 3-5-ETS1-1, 3-5-ETS1-2, 3-5-ETS1-3 1. Define engineering problems; 2. Design and test prototypes; 3. Iterate to improve solutions. 6-step engineering design process (Ask-Imagine-Plan-Create-Test-Improve); technology as anything humans make; iteration and failure as learning. Q1: 5 weeks (8/31–10/2) Paper Tower Challenge; Prototype iteration journal. PhET; Tinkercad; recycled materials for prototypes.
Forces & Motion Std NGSS 3-PS2-1 (Balanced/unbalanced forces); 3-PS2-2 (Motion patterns); 3-PS2-3 (Cause-effect force); 3-PS2-4 (Magnetic forces) 1. Investigate balanced & unbalanced forces; 2. Predict motion from patterns; 3. Explore magnets. Push and pull; gravity; motion and speed; balanced vs unbalanced forces; magnetic poles (N/S); attraction and repulsion. Q2: 5 weeks (10/19–11/20) Toy Car Track Test; Forces & motion quiz. PhET Forces & Motion; magnets; ramps; timers.
Simple Machines & Friction Std NGSS 3-PS2-2 (Motion patterns) 1. Identify the 6 simple machines; 2. Explain how they make work easier; 3. Investigate friction. Lever, wheel & axle, pulley, inclined plane, wedge, screw; friction on different surfaces; reducing/increasing friction. Q2: 4 weeks (11/23–12/18) Ramp Lifting Lab; Simple machines quiz. PhET Ramp; K'Nex kits; sandpaper/wax.
Life Cycles Std NGSS 3-LS1-1 (Life cycles) 1. Compare life cycles of plants and animals; 2. Explain metamorphosis; 3. Describe stages of the plant life cycle. Butterfly (egg-caterpillar-chrysalis-adult); frog (egg-tadpole-froglet-adult); chicken; plant seed-to-flower cycle. Q3: 3 weeks (1/7–1/29) Life-cycle poster project; Life cycles quiz. Butterfly kits; time-lapse videos; classroom terrarium.
Inherited Traits Std NGSS 3-LS3-1 (Inheritance); 3-LS3-2 (Environmental influence) 1. Distinguish inherited vs learned traits; 2. Understand DNA and genes at grade level; 3. Explore how environment can influence traits. Traits from parents; DNA and genes basics; inherited vs learned; sibling variation; plant traits. Q3: 3 weeks (2/1–2/19) Class Trait Survey; Inherited Traits quiz. Trait survey forms; Punnett-square intro; family photo lessons.
Hawaiian Ecosystems (Hawaiiana Unit) Std NGSS 3-LS2-1 (Groups & survival); 3-LS4-3 (Environment fit); 3-LS4-4 (Species change) 1. Identify endemic Hawaiian species; 2. Explain adaptations to Hawaiian habitats; 3. Understand threats and mālama ʻāina. Nēnē, honu, monk seal, honeycreepers; koa, ʻōhiʻa lehua, kukui; invasive species; reef/rainforest/coastal ecosystems. Q3: 3 weeks (2/22–3/12) Native Species Report; Hawaiiana quiz. Bishop Museum resources; DLNR pamphlets; native plant garden.
Weather Impacts Std NGSS 3-ESS2-1 (Weather data); 3-ESS2-2 (Climate patterns); 3-ESS3-1 (Weather hazards) 1. Track weather data; 2. Distinguish weather from climate; 3. Prepare for storms. Temperature, precipitation, wind, clouds (cumulus, stratus, cirrus); water cycle; Hawaiian trade winds; kau and hoʻoilo seasons; hurricane preparedness. Q4: 9 weeks (3/29–6/3) 5-day Weather Journal; Weather Impacts unit test. NOAA weather.gov; thermometers, rain gauges; Hurricane Iniki case study.
