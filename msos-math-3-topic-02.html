Lesson 1: 2s and 5s Facts

The 2s and 5s facts are the easiest to memorize. 2× a number is doubling. 5× a number always ends in 0 or 5. Learn these two families first — they help you figure out harder facts later.

Lesson 2: 10s Facts

Multiplying by 10 is the easiest fact ever: just put a 0 at the end! 10 × 7 = 70. 10 × 4 = 40. Any number times 10 ends in 0.

Lesson 3: 9s Facts (Finger Trick)

The 9s have a magic pattern: the digits always add to 9! 9 × 3 = 27 (2+7=9). 9 × 5 = 45 (4+5=9). Also, the first digit is always ONE LESS than the number you multiplied by. 9 × 4: first digit is 3, so 36.
