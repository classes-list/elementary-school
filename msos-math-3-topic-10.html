Lesson 1: Patterns with ×10, ×20, ×30

When you multiply by a multiple of 10, first multiply by the single digit, then add a 0. 4 × 30 = 4 × 3 × 10 = 12 × 10 = 120. Easy pattern!

Lesson 2: Multiplying Larger Multiples

The pattern works for any multiple of 10: 4 × 90 = 360. Practice with 60s, 70s, 80s, 90s. Watch the zero — it comes from the 10 factor.
